Legal & Rights › Understanding Parental Rights in Surrogacy: A Complete Nigerian Legal Guide Beginner
19 Jan 2025   4 min read

Navigating parental rights in surrogacy can be complex, especially within the Nigerian legal framework. This comprehensive guide breaks down everything you need to know about establishing and protecting your parental rights when pursuing surrogacy in Nigeria.

Legal Framework in Nigeria

Unlike some countries with explicit surrogacy legislation, Nigeria operates under a combination of common law principles and specific family law provisions. Understanding these frameworks is crucial for protecting your rights as intended parents.

The Birth Certificate Process

One of the most critical steps in establishing parental rights is ensuring your names appear on the birth certificate. This process typically involves coordination with your fertility clinic, legal counsel, and the relevant government registry offices.

The intended parents should work with their legal team to prepare all necessary documentation before the birth. This includes the surrogacy agreement, medical records confirming genetic parentage, and any court orders that may be required in your jurisdiction.

Protecting Your Rights

Beyond the initial legal framework, ongoing protection of parental rights requires careful attention to documentation and legal compliance. Working with experienced legal counsel throughout your surrogacy journey is not just recommended—it is essential for ensuring your rights are fully protected under Nigerian law.

Key Takeaway
  • In Nigeria, Intended Parents must be listed on the birth certificate. The surrogate has no automatic parental rights, but legal documentation is essential.
  • A properly drafted surrogacy agreement protects all parties and establishes clear intentions before conception.
